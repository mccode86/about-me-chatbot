Mario Christian is a music producer based in Indonesia who has been working professionally in the music industry since 2009. After more than fifteen years of creating music full-time, he began transitioning into AI engineering in early 2026, drawn in by a growing interest in large language models and the tools being built around them. He balances full-time music production work with intensive self-directed study in AI engineering.

Mario is based in the Greater Jakarta area of Indonesia. He was born on 18 November 1986. His native language is Indonesian, and he has strong working proficiency in English — particularly in reading and writing, as his code, documentation, and projects are all produced in English.

Mario holds a Bachelor of Law (S.H.) from Sam Ratulangi University in Manado, Indonesia, which he earned in 2010. Although his formal degree is in law, his path led toward music and, later, technology rather than legal practice.

Mario's roots in music trace back to high school, where he was a band vocalist and also worked as a DJ. At the time, clubs featured live bands alongside DJ sets, so he would often do both in a single night — fronting the band, then DJing, back and forth until early morning. He would get home near dawn, sleep about two hours, and still go to school — an intensity and work ethic that has stayed with him ever since.

Mario's path into music production began even earlier, in elementary school, through a PlayStation game called MTV Music Generator, which gave him an intuitive feel for how tracks are built. In his early university years, his older brother — a well-known DJ in Indonesia who won the Pioneer DJ Indonesia championship and represented Indonesia internationally, and who has since passed away — introduced him to the software Acid Pro. Having already absorbed the core concept years earlier, Mario picked it up immediately and began creating song mashups, setting him on the path toward professional music production.

Mario's music career began with dubstep production, inspired by artists like Skrillex, and his first commercially released track came out in 2013. Over the years he developed the ability to produce across virtually any genre, taking on whatever style a project demanded. Three years ago he was commissioned to produce trailer music for a club opening event and discovered a deep passion for the genre. Since then, trailer music has become his specialty and primary creative focus, with Hans Zimmer and Tom Holkenborg (Junkie XL) as his main artistic influences.

Mario works as a full-time music producer at Holywings Group — one of Indonesia's largest and fastest-growing entertainment and food-and-beverage companies, with dozens of venues across Indonesia — where he is known for delivering high-quality work at exceptional speed. Where colleagues typically spend one to two weeks on a single track, he completes most productions in two days. Trailer music pieces — his specialty — he finishes in a single day, while peers may spend several weeks on the same type of work. All output is approved by supervisors, and speed comes from preparation: he begins thinking through a task the night before so that execution the next day is focused and uninterrupted.

Mario first encountered Python in 2020 through an online Udemy course, shortly before the pandemic disrupted his momentum. In early 2026 he returned to programming, this time drawn in by Claude — Anthropic's AI assistant — after discovering AI engineering content on YouTube. Rather than following a conventional course format, he designed his own intensive curriculum with Claude's help: one Claude instance planned the full multi-stage syllabus; a second served as his interactive tutor in a terminal-based learning environment inside PyCharm. Within approximately four months he completed the full curriculum alongside a full-time job.

Mario's technical skills span the full AI engineering stack. He is proficient in Python, including object-oriented programming, data structures, file I/O, custom exceptions, and unit testing with pytest. On the data side he works with pandas and numpy for analysis and transformation. For AI application development he uses the Anthropic and OpenAI SDKs, builds retrieval-augmented generation pipelines with ChromaDB and OpenAI embeddings, structures AI outputs with Pydantic, and serves applications via FastAPI with async support. He stores persistent data in SQLite and uses Git and GitHub for version control throughout all projects.

Mario has implemented production-grade patterns across his projects, including structured logging, exponential-backoff retries for rate-limited API calls, per-call cost and latency tracking, and systematic evals for measuring AI output quality before and after prompt changes. He understands the trade-offs between synchronous and asynchronous request handling, and has deployed a live FastAPI application to Railway with environment variables managed securely outside the codebase.

Mario's first portfolio project is api-call-logger (github.com/mccode86/api-call-logger), a Python CLI tool that logs Claude API calls to a local JSON file, recording model name, input and output token counts, cost in USD, latency in milliseconds, a prompt excerpt, a response excerpt, and custom tags. The tool supports adding, removing, searching by model or tag, filtering by cost range, and viewing statistical summaries including model distribution and total cost per model.

Mario's second project is an API usage analytics dashboard built on top of the call logger. It loads the JSON logs into a pandas DataFrame, computes derived metrics such as cost per output token and calls per day, produces per-model aggregations for total cost and average latency, fetches a live USD-to-IDR exchange rate from a public currency API to convert spend figures into Indonesian Rupiah, and exports a formatted summary report as CSV.

Mario's third project is ai-cost-advisor (github.com/mccode86/ai-cost-advisor), a multi-turn CLI chatbot powered by the Anthropic SDK. It reads the API call logs, injects them as context into a system prompt, and answers natural-language questions about spending — for example, which model delivered the best cost per output token, what the most expensive call of the week was, or how latency varied across models. The chatbot maintains full conversation history across turns so follow-up questions resolve correctly.

Mario's capstone project is this About-Me Chatbot: a RAG-powered conversational interface that answers questions about his background, skills, and projects. The backend is built with FastAPI and deployed publicly on Railway. It combines ChromaDB for semantic search over personal documents, the Anthropic SDK for response generation, SQLite for logging each conversation turn with cost and latency data, and Pydantic for typed request and response models. A pytest test suite covers the core retrieval and generation logic.
